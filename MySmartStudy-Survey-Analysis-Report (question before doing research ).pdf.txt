1. Background of Problem  
First-year teacher trainees at IPG Kampus Perempuan Melayu Melaka face significant 
challenges in transitioning from the structured secondary school environment to the 
autonomous learning expected in tertiary education. To validate these challenges and 
inform the design of MySmartStudy, a comprehensive survey was conducted among 103 
first-year students across 9 classes (PI 2, PI 3, BM 3, BM KBT, SEJ, PK 2, TESL 1, TESL 3, BM 1) 
using a 30 -item online questionnaire.  
1.1 Digital Readiness of Students  
The survey revealed that 84% of respondents (87 out of 103) are comfortable or very 
comfortable with using digital technology for studying. Specifically, 34% are very 
comfortable and 50% are comfortable. Only 1% reported being slightly uncomfortable, 
while  15% remained neutral. This confirms strong digital readiness among the target users, 
validating that a digital learning platform would be well -received.  
 
Figure 1: Digital Technology Comfort Level Among Respondents (n=103)  
1.2 Poor Note Organisation and Memorisation Reliance  
A critical finding is that 88% of students (91 out of 103) sometimes, often, or very often rely 
on memorisation because their notes are not well organised. Only 12% rarely resort to 
memorisation. The breakdown: 61% sometimes, 23% often, and 4% very often. When 
asked about their biggest frustrations, the top responses were: difficulty finding 
information quickly (28%), too many files or notebooks (14%), and notes scattered across 
platforms (13%).  

 
Figure 2: Reliance on Memorisation Due to Poor Note Organisation (n=103)  
1.3 Thinking Map Usage Challenges  
While 64% of students report being confident or very confident in using Thinking Maps, 
74% have been unsure which template best suited their topic (68 sometimes + 8 
frequently). The main barriers to more frequent usage are time constraints (30 
respondents) , difficulty choosing the right map (29), and a combination of both (11). 
Furthermore, 70% face handwriting or space issues when drawing complex maps 
manually (58 sometimes + 14 often), confirming the need for a digital mind map editor.  
 
Figure 3: Confidence in Using Different Types of Thinking Maps (n=103)  

 
Figure 4: Handwriting and Space Issues with Manual Complex Maps (n=103)  
1.4 Collaboration and Group Work Challenges  
In group work, 82% of respondents have experienced duplicated work or version 
confusion (8 very often + 25 often + 51 sometimes). Encouragingly, 80% view real -time 
collaborative editing of Thinking Maps positively (65 positive + 17 very positive), and 90% 
find comment and feedback features on shared maps useful (61 useful + 32 very useful).

2. Design of the Product  
Based on the survey findings from 103 respondents, MySmartStudy was designed as a 
comprehensive multi -platform AI -enhanced Learning Management System. The system 
architecture and feature set were informed by the specific needs expressed by the 
students. Th is section presents the product highlights, system framework diagrams, and 
survey validation of key features.  
2.1 Development Methodology  
The Waterfall Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) was selected due to the fixed 
academic scope and structured documentation requirements of the Final Year Project. 
The methodology consists of five sequential phases: Requirement Analysis (informed by 
the 103-respondent survey), System Design, Implementation, Testing (unit, integration, 
and UAT), and Deployment & Maintenance.  
 
Figure 6: Waterfall SDLC Development Methodology  
2.2 System Architecture Framework  
MySmartStudy is built on a hybrid multi -tier architecture consisting of six interconnected 
layers: the User Layer (students, lecturers, admin), the Frontend Layer (Next.js 16 web app 
and Flutter mobile app), the API Gateway (FastAPI with 41 REST API router s), the Service 
Layer (authentication, courses, maps, quizzes, gradebook, analytics, AI, and messaging 
modules), the Data Store (Firebase Firestore with Firebase Authentication), and the AI 
Engine (Google Gemini 2.5 Flash with ChromaDB for RAG).  

 
Figure 7: MySmartStudy System Architecture Framework  
  

2.3 User Process Flow  
The user process flowchart illustrates how students and lecturers interact with the system 
from registration through to continuous learning. After authentication via Firebase Auth + 
JWT, the system routes users based on their role (student or lecturer) to their respective 
dashboards. Students progress through course enrollment, material access, assignment 
submission, mind map creation, AI study tools, and gamification tracking. Lecturers 
manage courses, resources, assignments, reviews, AI -assisted evaluatio n, and analytics. 
Both roles interact through real -time collaboration features and share a common 
Firebase Firestore cloud sync layer.  
 
Figure 8: MySmartStudy User Process Flow  

  
2.4 AI -Powered Features Workflow  
MySmartStudy integrates seven AI -powered features through a centralized AI Feature 
Router. When a user submits a request, the router directs it to the appropriate AI module: 
Study Companion (chat -based academic support), Study Materials (auto -generated 
summaries, flashcards, practice quizzes), Exam Planner (personalized study schedules), 
Mind Map Buddy (RAG -based node suggestions), Plagiarism Detection (similarity scoring 
for lecturers), Grading Assistant (AI -recommended grades and feedback), and 
Timetable Analysis (schedule conflict detection). All modules are powered by the Google 
Gemini 2.5 Flash AI engine with ChromaDB providing RAG capabilities for contextual 
responses.  
 
Figure 9: AI -Powered Features Workflow  
2.5 Product Highlights  
Key features designed in direct response to survey data:  
• Collaborative Mind Map Editor with 15+ templates, real -time collaboration, presence 
indicators, and AI node suggestions via RAG — addressing the 74% who are unsure which 
template to use and 70% facing handwriting/space issues.  
• AI Study Companion powered by Google Gemini 2.5 Flash — supporting the 88% who rely 
on memorisation.  
• AI-Generated Study Materials (summaries, flashcards, practice quizzes) — addressing the 
need for better note organisation.  

• Gamification Engine with 12 auto -awarded badges, login streaks, participation scoring — 
responding to the 80% who find gamification motivating.  
• Complete LMS Suite (courses, assignments, quizzes, gradebook, attendance, forums, chat, 
messaging, calendar) — replacing fragmented tools.  
• Lecturer Analytics Dashboard with at -risk student identification and submission trend 
monitoring.  
• Progress Tracking Dashboard — responding to the 78% who say it would help manage 
studies.  
2.6 Survey Validation of Key Features  
The survey data strongly validates the feature design. 83% find automatic Thinking Map 
recommendation helpful or very helpful. 95% believe pre -filled templates would save 
time. 80% find gamification motivating. 78% confirm progress tracking would help.

3.2 Impact on Lecturers  
MySmartStudy provides lecturers with real -time analytics dashboards that track student 
engagement, submission trends, and course completion rates. The at -risk student 
identification feature enables early intervention before poor performance manifests in 
formal assessments. AI -powered plagiarism detection and grading assistance improve 
evaluation efficiency and consistency. The mind map review interface allows lecturers to 
assess student understanding through visual knowledge representations.  
3.3 Impact on the Institution  

With 84% of students already digitally comfortable and 89% willing to participate in pilot 
testing and recommend the platform, the institutional adoption potential is very high. The 
centralized platform replaces fragmented tools, standardizes digital learn ing practices, 
and produces future educators who are digitally literate. The audit logging system 
ensures accountability, while the admin dashboard provides oversight.

3.4 Summary  
The survey data from 103 respondents provides strong empirical validation for 
MySmartStudy. Key statistics confirm that the identified problems are real and 
widespread (88% memorisation reliance, 82% version confusion, 74% template 
uncertainty), the propos ed solutions match user needs (83% want AI recommendations, 
80% want gamification, 95% want pre -filled templates), and adoption readiness is high 
(84% digitally comfortable, 89% willing to pilot and recommend). MySmartStudy is a data -
driven response to doc umented challenges in teacher training education.
